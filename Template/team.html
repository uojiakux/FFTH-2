Our Team

Board of Directors

Chief/Sir Uche Jim Ojiaku (Jim)

Founder/President

Jim graduated from the Department of Electrical Engineering (Institute of Technology) of the University of Minnesota. Since college, Jim has held extensive and varied engineering roles among leading U.S. companies, including Intel, Honeywell, Medtronic, Smiths Medical, Guidant, Boston Scientific, and ACIST Medical Systems. Jim is a prolific reader/writer, with a great interest in cultural activities and promoting inter-cultural relationships. Additionally, he is a traditional chief – a title he inherited from his father (Late Chief/Sir Anthony Ojiaku), as well as a fourth-degree member of the Knights of Columbus.

Dr. Obinna Ojiaku

Director

Obinna received his Bachelor of Science (BS) degree in Biochemistry from St. Cloud State University in 2008. During his academic tenure at St. Cloud State, he was involved in multiple student organizations including Organization for the Prevention of AIDS in Africa (OPAA), African Students Association, and Medical Professions Association.

Obinna received his Doctor of Pharmacy (Pharm.D) from Mercer University College of Pharmacy in Atlanta in 2016. During his academic career at Mercer, he was involved in student organizations such as Kappa Psi Pharmaceutical Fraternity Inc, Georgia Society of Health Systems Pharmacists, Academy of Managed Care Pharmacists, and Council of Students. He currently works as a Pharmacy Manager at CVS Pharmacy in Macon, GA. In his free time, he enjoys staying active by working out, traveling, and hanging out with friends. His future goals include giving back by being able to travel the world to provide pharmaceutical care to underdeveloped areas.

Mrs. Ogochukwu N. Ojiaku, MSN, RN, BSN FNP-C

Director

Ogochukwu graduated from Chamberlain College of Nursing Addison Illinois in 2016, with a Master of Science degree in Nursing, with a specialty in Family Nurse Practitioner. Prior to obtaining her Masters’ Degree, she graduated from North Georgia College and State University with Associate degree in Nursing, after which she attended Georgia Southwestern University for her Bachelor of Science in Nursing degree.

She is currently employed as a Nurse Practitioner, with the Veterans Affairs Medical Center in Atlanta Georgia. She also works part time for the state of Georgia agency, Mcintosh Trail Community Service Board, where she provides mental health services to the rural community. As a registered nurse, she has worked in various settings and capacities of nursing, including acute care, inpatient hospice, home hospice, Oncology and palliative care. Ogochukwu is a caring wife to her supportive husband of almost 18 years, and a devoted mother of 4 children. She enjoys caring for others and is greatly interested in inter-cultural activities. When not working, she enjoys quality time with her family, cooking, shopping and reading.

Mr. Emodi Amagwula, BS, MA

Director

Emodi obtained his BS in Biochemistry from Murray State University, Murray Kentucky and went on to obtain his MA degree in Urban & Regional Planning. He holds his 4-Year Letterman (Soccer), and 3-Year Letterman (Football) trophies. He is the Organizing Secretary for Globelink Organization Inc. Most importantly, Emodi has a deep passion for supporting the cause for the poor and needy in the South-Eastern region of Nigeria, with many years of experience devoted to work in this area.
