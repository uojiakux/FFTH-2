Our Mission

A disease-free and better-nourished body helps to prolong life.

Our Overall Mission is to: Establish a Malaria Treatment and Prevention Center in the Oru region of South-Eastern Nigeria, which will help to minimize the massive number of deaths caused by malaria (see Our Story for more details). The goal is to ensure that Oru Region has a Malaria treatment and education Center that includes a medical unit that provides for both preventive and corrective measures, as well as an educational unit that provides excellent information on common knowledge about malaria.

Based on the fact that a disease-free and better-nourished body helps to prolong life, a sub-component of this mission is to provide food for the poor and needy in the impoverished areas of Oru region of South-Eastern Nigeria, while assisting them to become independent and selfsustaining.

Milestones for the Mission:

  • Commence construction of the Malaria Treatment and Prevention Center on/before January, 2020, with a target completion date of December 2024. Also plan to have the center become self-sustaining within three years of opening to public. Self-sustaining means "no outside support for existence and operation".
  • Create a poultry process that will be selfsustaining within five years.
  • Create a piggery process that will be selfsustaining within five years.
  • Create a farming infrastructure that helps feed the needy and poor, but which will be selfsustaining within five years.
  • Identify talented students who can be sponsored to obtain university education in Agriculture or related fields, and who, upon completion of education, can serve as vanguards to help in transforming people from indigents to productive citizens.
